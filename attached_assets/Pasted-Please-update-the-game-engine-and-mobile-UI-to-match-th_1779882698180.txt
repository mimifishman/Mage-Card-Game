Please update the game engine and mobile UI to match the following rules.
There are six separate areas that need to change. Read all of them before
starting — some interact with each other.

---

## Rule 1: Attacker chooses which Royals attack

**Current behaviour:** When a player declares an attack, every eligible Royal
in their Court is automatically sent into battle.

**Correct rule:** The attacking player decides which of their Royals will
attack. They do not have to attack with all of them. Royals that are not sent
into the attack stay back and remain available to defend on the opponent's
next turn.

**What needs to change:**
- The declare_attack action must carry a list of the specific Royal card IDs
  the player has chosen to attack with.
- The server must validate that every Royal in that list is in the attacker's
  Court, is not haste-locked, and has not already attacked this turn.
- Only the chosen Royals are marked as having attacked. The rest are
  unaffected.
- The player must choose at least one Royal. An empty selection is an error.
- In the mobile UI, tapping the Attack button should start a selection step
  where the player taps individual Royals in their Court to mark them for
  the attack. Royals that are ineligible (haste-locked or already attacked)
  cannot be selected. After selecting at least one Royal, the player taps a
  confirm button and then picks a target opponent.

---

## Rule 2: Multiple blockers can gang up on one attacker

**Current behaviour:** Each attacking Royal can only be blocked by one
defending Royal (one-to-one mapping).

**Correct rule:** A defending player may commit several of their Royals to
block the same attacking Royal — they gang up on it together. However, each
defending Royal can only be assigned to one attacker; once a Royal is
blocking, it cannot also be used to block a different attacker that turn.

When multiple blockers face one attacker, damage works as follows:
- The attacking player decides the order in which their attacker's damage
  is assigned to the blockers.
- Lethal damage (enough to kill a blocker) must be fully assigned to one
  blocker before any damage can be assigned to the next.
- Each blocker independently deals its own attack value back to the attacker.
- Example: A 5-attack Royal faces blockers of 1 HP and 2 HP. The attacker
  assigns 1 damage to the first blocker (killing it), then 2 damage to the
  second (killing it). Both blockers hit the attacker back (1+2=3 damage).
  The attacker could also choose to assign all 5 to the first blocker, leaving
  the second blocker untouched — but that wastes damage.

**What needs to change:**
- The blocking data model must allow multiple blocker IDs per attacking Royal,
  not just one.
- The confirm_declare_blocks action must support this: for each attacker, the
  defender provides either a list of one or more blocker Royal IDs, or a pass.
- The same blocker Royal cannot be assigned to more than one attacker.
- After blockers are confirmed, the attacking player must be given the
  opportunity to set the damage assignment order for any attacker that has
  multiple blockers. This is an ordered list — which blocker takes damage
  first, second, and so on.
- The damage assignment step should happen before the Duel phase begins (or
  as part of the attacker's first Duel action).
- Combat resolution must apply damage in the attacker's declared order,
  assigning lethal damage to each blocker in sequence before moving to the
  next, with any excess damage being lost (it does not carry through to the
  defending player).
- In the mobile UI, the blocking modal must allow the defending player to
  assign multiple Royals to one attacker. After blocks are submitted, the
  attacker must be shown a screen where they drag or tap to set the damage
  order for each multi-blocked attacker.

## Rule 3: Card cancellation — equal buffs and debuffs go to the Abyss

**Current behaviour:** This mechanic does not exist in the code at all.

**Correct rule:** When the total positive buff from Spade cards and the total
negative debuff from Club cards attached to a Royal cancel out mathematically
to a net of zero, those cancelling cards are automatically removed from the
Royal and sent to the Abyss.

How it works:
- Spade cards attached to a Royal add equally to both attack and health.
- Club cards debuff equally from both attack and health.
- If the sum of all attached Spade pip values equals the sum of all attached
  Club pip values on a Royal, those Spade and Club cards have cancelled each
  other out. They are removed from the Royal and discarded to the Abyss.
- Heart cards are not involved in cancellation. They only affect health and
  have no equal-opposite counterpart.
- The Royal itself remains in play with its base stats (plus any remaining
  uncancelled buffs).
- Cancellation is checked automatically any time a Spade or Club is attached
  to a Royal. If a cancellation is triggered, the cards are sent to the Abyss
  immediately as part of the same action.
- This is intentionally strategic: a player can deliberately create a
  cancellation to move cards into the Abyss so they can later be recalled
  with a Spade discard.

Example: A Royal has a 3-pip Spade (+3 ATK/+3 HP) and a 2-pip Club
(-2 ATK/-2 HP) attached. Total Spade pips = 3, total Club pips = 2.
No cancellation yet. Now a 1-pip Club (-1 ATK/-1 HP) is attached. Total
Club pips = 3. Now Spade total equals Club total (both 3). The 3-pip Spade
and both Clubs (2-pip and 1-pip) are all sent to the Abyss. The Royal
returns to its base stats.

**What needs to change:**
- After any Spade or Club card is attached to a Royal, the server must check
  whether the total pip value of all attached Spades equals the total pip
  value of all attached Clubs on that Royal.
- If they are equal, all attached Spade cards and all attached Club cards on
  that Royal are removed and added to the Abyss as part of the same action
  response. Heart cards attached to the same Royal are not removed.
- The Royal's buffAttack and buffHealth must be recalculated to reflect only
  the remaining uncancelled attachments.
- No new action or player input is needed — cancellation is automatic and
  immediate.

## Rule 4: Allow Jokers in the respond-to-club window

**Current behaviour:** During the respond-to-club window, the targeted player
is blocked from playing both Royals and Jokers.

**Correct rule:** The targeted player may play any card EXCEPT a Royal and
EXCEPT a Diamond to the Mine. In particular, Jokers ARE allowed (a player may
Joker the attacking Royal, or deal direct damage, in response to a Club). This
makes the respond-to-club window consistent with the duel window, which
already allows Jokers.

**What needs to change:**
- Remove the rule that blocks Jokers during the respond-to-club window, in both
  the server-side validation and the mobile UI's valid-actions logic.
- Keep the existing blocks: no Royals, and no Diamonds played to the Mine.
- Diamonds may still be discarded for draw or boost during the window, subject
  to the one-Diamond-per-turn limit (this is already correct).

---

## Rule 5: An attacking Royal stays tapped through the opponent's turn

**Current behaviour:** When a Royal attacks it is marked as having attacked,
but that status is cleared at the end of every turn for all players. This means
a Royal that attacked is incorrectly available to block during the opponent's
turn.

**Correct rule:** Attacking taps a Royal (turns it sideways). A tapped Royal
cannot block or defend during the opponent's turn. It stays tapped through the
opponent's turn(s) and only untaps at the start of its own controller's next
turn. Blocking does NOT tap a Royal — a Royal that blocked is still free to
attack on its controller's next turn.

The trade-off: committing a Royal to attack leaves it unable to defend until
the attacking player's next turn comes around.

**What needs to change:**
- A Royal that attacked must remain tapped (unable to be assigned as a blocker)
  for the entire duration until its controller's next turn begins.
- The untap should happen at the START of a player's own turn, applied only to
  that player's Royals — not at the end of every turn for everyone.
- The end-of-turn healing (resetting combat damage on all Royals) should stay
  as it is, but it must NOT untap a Royal that attacked. Only the controller's
  own untap step clears the attacked/tapped status.
- The blocking logic must reject any attempt to assign a tapped Royal (one that
  attacked and has not yet untapped) as a blocker.
- In the mobile UI, tapped Royals should be visually distinct and should not be
  selectable as blockers during the blocking phase.

## Rule 6: Destroying a Royal never costs the owner life

**Current behaviour:** When a Royal is destroyed by a Club (debuffed to 0 HP)
or by a Joker's destroy mode, the owner loses life equal to the Royal's base
health plus its health buffs.

**Correct rule:** Life is ONLY lost when damage is dealt directly to a player.
Destroying a Royal — by ANY means — never causes its owner to lose life. The
destroyed Royal simply goes to the Abyss along with its attached cards.

Life can only be lost from:
- An unblocked attacker dealing combat damage directly to a player.
- A Club played in direct-damage mode (deal its pip value to a player).
- A Joker played in damage mode (deal 10 to a player).

**What needs to change:**
- Remove the life-loss calculation when a Club debuff brings a Royal to 0 HP.
  The Royal goes to the Abyss; no life is deducted from its owner.
- Remove the life-loss calculation when a Joker destroys a Royal. The Royal
  goes to the Abyss; no life is deducted from its owner.
- Combat death already correctly deducts no life — leave that as is.

---

## Important: when can you play cards on the opponent's turn?

You may ONLY play a card during the opponent's turn in these two situations:

1. **Your Royal is being debuffed by a Club** and you want to save it. This is
   the respond-to-club window. Only the targeted player may react, and only
   with non-Royal cards. They cannot play a Diamond to the Mine (Diamonds may
   still be discarded for draw or boost, subject to the one-Diamond-per-turn
   limit). Jokers ARE allowed.
2. **You are being attacked.** During the blocking and duel phases, the player
   under attack (and the attacker) may play cards to influence combat.

There is NO general ability to play cards during the opponent's normal main
phase. Outside of the two reactive windows above, only the active player may
play cards, and only during their own main phase.

The only change needed here: the respond-to-club window currently blocks
Jokers. Allow Jokers in that window (see Rule 4 below). Everything else about
when cards can be played is already correct — please do NOT widen it.

---

## Rules that are already correct — do not change these

The following are implemented correctly and should not be touched:
- Reactive play is limited to the two windows described above (respond-to-club
  and being attacked) — do not widen this.
- The Duel phase (alternating turns for both players to play cards during
  combat before damage resolves).
- The respond_to_club window (defender gets to react before a Club debuff
  lands) — keep this, but allow Jokers in it per Rule 4.
- The discard phase (player must discard to hand limit of 7 at end of turn).
- All Royals healing fully at end of turn (combat damage reset) — keep this,
  but per Rule 5 the heal must NOT untap a Royal that attacked.
- Haste lock (a Royal cannot attack the turn it is played).
- The one Diamond action per turn limit, and Diamonds-to-Mine / Royals being
  playable only on your own turn.
- The Joker (destroys any Royal or deals 10 damage to a player, costs 10 Vault).
- The one-attack-per-turn limit (hasAttackedThisTurn on the game state).
- No Royal destruction ever costs life (combat, Club, or Joker) — see Rule 6.
  Life is lost ONLY from direct damage to a player: unblocked combat damage,
  Club direct-damage mode, and Joker damage mode.
